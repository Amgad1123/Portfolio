Twitter Bioinformatics project

This is my Twitter Bioinformatics project. I worked alongside a mentor through the first year of my undergraduate degree to complete this project. I worked 10 hours a week and met once a week or as needed with my Mentor. Using several parameters, the main objective was to find the relationship between tweets and retweets from UNMC accounts. The parameters included using the keyword virus, over a span of one year (January 2021-January 2022), and the original tweet had to come from a UNMC Twitter account.

For data-scraping we used a website named Sprinklr that allowed us to filter tweets based on the set parameters. Sprinklr produced the results into two CSV files (one tweet and the other retweets). The rest of the time was used to construct a data pipeline of the two CSV files into one CSV file organizing the original tweet and subtweets based on the earliest time posted. This in turn would essentially provide a chain of tweets. This was done using Python, Node.js, and frameworks including Pandas and Numpy.

The finished result was an Excel file that contained the chain of tweets and retweets. We extracted the location from the retweets to analyze where retweeters were from. We concluded that a majority of the retweets from UNMC accounts occur from individuals located in surrounding cities and states of Lincoln, Nebraska, with some small instances occurring in larger cities such as New York and California.
